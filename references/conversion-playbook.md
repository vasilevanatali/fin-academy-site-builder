# Conversion Playbook

Use this reference when the page goal is registration, payment, lead, consultation request, or conversion improvement.

## Conversion Rule

Conversion is not guaranteed by design alone. It depends on offer, audience, price, traffic quality, trust, timing, and page execution.

The skill should improve the page by reducing confusion, distrust, friction, and missed next steps.

## Conversion Strategy Before Layout

Before writing/coding, define:

- primary conversion: registration, payment, application, Telegram click, request, call;
- secondary conversion: ask a question, save page, view program, open tariff;
- audience stage: cold, warm, current student, returning buyer;
- decision barrier: trust, price, time, level, relevance, payment method, fear of AI/tools;
- proof available: screenshots, examples, author credentials, reviews, public links;
- strongest next step: pay now, register, get recording, join waitlist, book call.

If any item is missing and affects the page, ask one concise question or mark `данных нет`.

## Above-The-Fold Test

The first viewport must answer in 5 seconds:

- what is this?
- who is it for?
- what task/result does it help with?
- what will I get?
- what should I click?
- why should I trust this page enough to continue?

If the answer is not visible, fix hero before polishing lower sections.

## Offer Clarity

Use concrete offer language:

- `мини-урок`, `эфир`, `запись`, `экскурсия`, `консультация`, `практикум`, `курс`;
- audience in the headline or support copy;
- price/date/access near CTA when relevant;
- result framed as a practical job, not a promise of guaranteed income.

Avoid:

- "новый уровень", "прорыв", "освойте будущее";
- unverified ROI claims;
- hidden price for low-ticket products unless requested.

## Trust Ladder

Build trust before asking for payment or personal data.

Use:

- real author credentials;
- real screenshots/artifacts;
- public example sites;
- transparent price/access/payment terms;
- contact links;
- legal/footer links;
- FAQ for objections;
- "what is included" list;
- realistic limits: who this is not for.

NN/g-style trust factors to cover:

- design quality;
- up-front disclosure;
- comprehensive/current content;
- connection to the rest of the web.

## CTA System

Use one primary CTA label per page type:

- `Зарегистрироваться на эфир`;
- `Получить доступ`;
- `Забрать запись`;
- `Выбрать тариф`;
- `Оставить заявку`;
- `Записаться на консультацию`.

Rules:

- repeat CTA after major decision sections;
- keep CTA visually consistent;
- put payment/access note close to CTA;
- preserve UTM parameters;
- fire analytics goals without blocking navigation;
- on mobile, show sticky CTA after first viewport.

Avoid:

- `Подробнее` as primary CTA;
- multiple competing primary CTAs;
- CTA without destination or placeholder note.

## Pricing And Payment

For paid pages:

- show price, access term, and included value together;
- explain payment methods only if confirmed;
- keep legal entity/invoice/installment notes accurate;
- show what happens after payment;
- keep one-off offer payment block focused.

For several tariffs:

- compare by buyer job, not arbitrary feature count;
- make the recommended tariff only if confirmed;
- keep mobile cards/tabs readable;
- avoid wide comparison tables.

## Friction Audit

Before delivery, check:

- unclear headline;
- weak proof before payment;
- hidden price;
- too many CTAs;
- CTA below the fold only;
- no mobile sticky CTA;
- long program without artifacts;
- form asks for unnecessary data;
- no explanation for required phone/Telegram/email;
- payment link loses UTM;
- page looks generic and could belong to any course.

## Forms And Lead Capture

Use forms only when needed. For simple pages, Telegram/payment/order links may convert better than a custom form.

If form is needed:

- ask minimum fields;
- label fields clearly;
- show why phone/email is needed;
- validate without deleting data;
- make error messages specific;
- keep submit button visible;
- confirm what happens after submit.

## Conversion Handoff

When handing off a conversion page, include:

- primary conversion goal;
- main CTA;
- proof used;
- missing facts;
- risks;
- what was checked;
- 3-5 next test hypotheses.
