# Trend Radar 2026

Use this reference when the user asks for a site to feel current, premium, fresh, trend-aware, or not generic.

## Rule

Use trends as tools, not decoration. A trend is allowed only when it improves at least one of these:

- clarity of the offer;
- trust;
- perceived expertise;
- comparison of options;
- speed to CTA;
- mobile usability;
- memory of the page.

Do not add a trend if it makes the page slower, less readable, harder to maintain, or less credible.

## 2026 Direction For Fin Academy Sites

### Outcome-Oriented UX

Design for the visitor's job, not for a pretty screen. Each section should answer:

- what can I do after this product?
- what will I receive?
- what is the next decision?
- what risk is reduced?

Use for: Codex/Claude/AI lessons, finance tools, consulting offers.

### Human-Curated AI

AI pages should not look like generic AI output. Show human curation:

- real instructor notes;
- prompt examples;
- before/after artifacts;
- screenshots of working tools;
- curated routes through complex AI tools;
- "what we checked" blocks.

Avoid: over-polished abstract AI waves, fake neural backgrounds, generic robot metaphors.

### Adaptive, Not Random Personalization

Use lightweight personalization only when the branch is meaningful:

- audience tabs: финансист / собственник / эксперт / команда;
- CTA copy based on tariff or intent;
- UTM-preserved payment paths;
- content anchors by user job.

Do not build opaque AI personalization, hidden scoring, or fake scarcity.

### Tactile Digital Craft

Add warmth and specificity through:

- real screenshots;
- document/workbook previews;
- hand-marked callouts;
- layered paper/table/artifact motifs;
- subtle texture in backgrounds;
- editorial captions;
- human photos.

Avoid generic stock photos and sterile "AI SaaS" gradients.

### Motion With Purpose

Use motion to clarify state or reveal structure:

- progressive reveal for long program sections;
- micro-interactions on CTAs/cards;
- before/after slider;
- artifact tabs;
- count-up only for verified numbers;
- smooth anchor navigation.

Respect reduced motion. Do not animate every block.

### Dark Anchors, Not Dark Soup

Use dark/high-contrast sections for:

- tariff/payment block;
- final CTA;
- proof/authority;
- strategic transition.

Do not make the whole page dark if it hurts reading or makes screenshots/product previews less clear.

### Dense But Scannable Executive UX

For finance/executive audiences, prefer:

- clear tables/cards;
- short paragraphs;
- meaningful headings;
- proof close to claims;
- prices and terms visible;
- no vague motivational copy.

The page should feel like a good business document turned into a web experience.

## Trend Use By Product Type

### AI Excursion / Tool Lesson

Use:

- route map;
- tool screenshots;
- prompt previews;
- before/after outputs;
- "what you will be able to do" tabs;
- interactive artifact gallery.

Avoid:

- vague "discover AI possibilities" pages;
- futuristic decoration without examples.

### Paid Recording / Mini-Course

Use:

- lesson/player preview;
- modules as timeline;
- clear access terms;
- artifact bundle;
- tariff/payment card;
- FAQ about access and level.

Avoid:

- hiding price;
- bloated long-form program without concrete outputs.

### Consulting / Outsourcing

Use:

- process map;
- diagnostic checklist;
- scope boundaries;
- deliverables;
- case-like examples;
- trust block with responsibility and communication rules.

Avoid:

- course-style hype;
- too many playful visuals.

### Webinar / Live Event

Use:

- expert-led hero;
- date/time visible;
- speaker proof;
- agenda with outcomes;
- registration block repeated;
- reminder of recording/access if confirmed.

Avoid:

- fake urgency and fake live seats.

## 2026 Must-Haves

- Mobile-first at 390px.
- Core Web Vitals readiness: LCP, INP, CLS.
- Accessible target sizes and visible focus states.
- Local critical fonts/assets where possible.
- No external font blocking on school landing pages unless explicitly approved.
- Trust signals before payment/registration.
- Real artifacts, not generic illustrations.
- UTM survival through all payment/order/cross-sell links.
- Post-launch hypothesis list.

## Trend Anti-Patterns

Do not use:

- "trend salad": 3D + glass + neon + grain + AI orbs + heavy motion;
- fake personalization;
- chatbots or AI assistants without a real support process;
- carousels for critical proof;
- tiny low-contrast editorial text;
- heavy WebGL/3D on simple conversion pages;
- parallax that breaks mobile;
- dark blurred stock images as the main product signal;
- huge AI-generated illustrations with no product detail.
